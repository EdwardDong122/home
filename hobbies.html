My Website (Puzzles)

For Puzzles, I got into playing the Rubik's cube, or as we call it, "Speed Cubing", as we solve for a fast time.

I first started 'cubing' February of 2019, when I saw a friend cubing. He
was averaging around 14 seconds per solve, and I thought I needed plenty of time
to practice in order to reach his level. During that month, I needed to use a
cheat sheet to solve, and it still took me 3 months. But very quickly, I started
improving, and after a year, I got my all time highest, to this day. I got
a single solve time of 6.0, which really surprised my friends. I would say that
1 year is a pretty small amount of time to drop my personal best by 30 times.

Here is a table showing my how my average and personal best gradually became better up to May.

My Favourite Puzzles and Games

For board games, I recently got into chess, during the pandemic.

I created my account on Chess.com a few years ago, I took a few lessons, but at the time,
I wasn't very interested and didn't take much information in. I got back into chess
in grade 8 when we re-learned in class, and we had a competition. I didn't perform the
best, but this was how I started getting better. I currently play online, not
many over the board games, but Chess.com rating is very similar to competition ratings.
I will be listing my ratings on a table. In terms of Blitz and Bullet, these are time controls
that give you a certain amount of time to play your moves. There may be increment, but I usually
don't play with increment. Blitz time control means 3 minutes to 10 minutes for you, and bullet
can range from 30 seconds to less than 3 minutes. There is also a rating for chess puzzles,
where you try and solve chess problems, in which you try and get the best position possible,
whether it is checkmating the opponent or winning material.
